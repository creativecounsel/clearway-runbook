# Legal Reviewing Guidelines

For Legal (or whoever is reviewing) when a request doesn't have a Clearway match.

## Triage on intake

1. **Does it match a Clearway entry, even loosely?**
   - Exact/close paraphrase → no review needed, point requestor back to Clearway.
   - Close but expanded or more specific → Fast Lane.
   - No match → assess novelty (below).

2. **How novel is it?**
   - Minor variation on something cleared before → Fast Lane (target: 1 business day).
   - New data use, new claims category, new jurisdiction, new partner type, or anything regulatory → Full Review (target: 3–5 business days), loop in relevant SME.

3. **Is there a real deadline?**
   - If yes and tight, prioritize accordingly — but don't let urgency substitute for actually checking the claim.

## Reviewing principles

- **Use the comment label system** (`comment-labels.md`) for every comment, so the drafter knows exactly what's required versus optional.
- **Explain risk in plain terms**, not just "this is a problem." Say what the actual exposure is and what would fix it.
- **Offer an alternative, not just a rejection.** "No" without a path forward sends the requestor back to square one; "not this, but this instead" keeps momentum.
- **If you clear something new, add it to Clearway.** A one-off approval that never becomes a reusable entry means the same question comes back next quarter.
- **Log the request** (`/workshop/request-log.md` or your ticketing system) so patterns are visible later — repeated non-Clearway requests in the same category are a signal you need a new entry, not another ad hoc review.
