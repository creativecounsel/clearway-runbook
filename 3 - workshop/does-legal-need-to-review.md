# Does Legal Need to Review This?

A short test to make the call with confidence, instead of defaulting to "submit everything" or guessing.

## The test

**Does your asset:**
- Introduce a new product claim, solution name, or bundled use case?
- Claim outcomes related to privacy, security, fraud, or compliance?
- Use new performance metrics, statistics, or benchmark comparisons?
- Use third-party logos, names, partner brands, or customer references?
- Reveal technical implementation details that could have IP implications?

**Could or will your asset:**
- Be read as implying a guarantee, approval, or regulatory determination?
- Be read as describing automated decision-making or eligibility screening?
- Appear in a high-visibility channel (homepage, press, a major campaign, executive communications)?

**One more check:**
- Did a first-pass review tool, template, or another reviewer flag anything you haven't been able to resolve yourself?

## If none of the above applies

You can proceed without a full legal review. Before you publish:

1. Check your draft one more time against the pre-approved language in this runbook, so it matches what's already been cleared.
2. Keep a record of whatever self-check or first-pass tool output you used — attach it to the asset or log it in your tracking system, so there's a trail if a question comes up later.

## The tie-breaker

If you're genuinely unsure whether any of the above applies — submit it anyway. A quick, unnecessary review is cheaper than an unreviewed asset that turns out to need one.
