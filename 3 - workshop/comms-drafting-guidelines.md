# Comms Drafting Guidelines

For anyone drafting external-facing language that doesn't have a Clearway entry.

## Before you draft

1. Check `/ground-floor/clearway` — is there already approved language for this?
2. If close but not exact, draft using the closest Clearway entry as your starting point, and flag the deviation when you submit for review (this speeds up review — reviewers can focus on what changed).
3. If there's no relevant entry at all, draft from scratch using the principles below, and expect a fuller review.

## Drafting principles

- **Check your draft against `language-patterns-to-watch.md`** before submitting — it covers the most common avoidable risk patterns.
- **Avoid superlatives and comparatives** ("best," "most secure," "only") unless you have a verified, current basis for the claim.
- **Match tense to reality.** Don't describe something as live if it's in beta; don't describe a partnership as active if the agreement isn't executed.

## What to include when you submit for review

Use the intake template (`/workshop/intake-request.md` or your team's ticketing system):
- What you're publishing and where
- What's new about it, specifically
- What you already checked in Clearway, and why it didn't fully cover this
