## Five things to internalize first

- The company provides inputs, data, or tools; the customer or end user makes the actual decision. This explains most guardrails.
- Don't guarantee outcomes or use absolute language ("always," "never," "guarantees," "eliminates") unless a specific, narrow exception has been cleared.
- Every product/feature has a precise, defined meaning. Don't blur products together, especially when describing bundled solutions or use cases.
- Include a qualifier (e.g., "up to") and a source with every number or stat.
- When in doubt, run it through whatever first-pass review tool your team uses before submitting to Legal — catching obvious issues early speeds up the real review.
